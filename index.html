About Me

I am a visiting scholar at Stony Brook University (SBU), working in the Advanced Combustion and Energy Systems (ACES) Laboratory. I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from SBU in December 2024, with my dissertation titled: Understanding the Role of the Internal Combustion Engine for a Hybrid Solid Oxide Fuel Cell Power Generation System. The respective work pertains to the hybridization of internal combustion engines with solid oxide fuel cells (SOFC), and in particular to the combustion of the latter's anode off-gas in an engine downstream of the SOFC. I additionally graduated from Delft University of Technology with a M.Sc. in Sustainable Energy Technology in June 2017. My M.Sc. thesis is titled: Determination of Fatigue Assessment of Monopile - Based Offshore Wind Turbines through Fidelity Quantification, and involves the examination of different tools in identifying the fatigue damage of offshore wind turbines' monopiles. Prior to that, I graduated from the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA) in 2014 with a B.Eng. and a M.Eng. in Mechanical Engineering. My thesis in NTUA's respective program is associated with reviewing net-zero energy buildings. For more information you can go through my resume.

Research Interests

My research interests are mostly associated with the performed studies and line of work. Resulting from all internal combustion engines, solid oxide fuel cells, wind turbines (wake fields & fatigue damage) and energy efficient buidlings, my focus is on:

  • Applied Thermodynamics
  • Heat Transfer Analysis
  • Computational Fluid Dynamics
  • Advanced Combustion
  • Chemical Kinetics
  • Fatigue Damage
