About Me

I am a visiting scholar at Stony Brook University, working in the Advanced Combustion and Energy Systems (ACES) Laboratory. I reside in the United States since first joining the Ph.D. Program in Mechanical Engineering of Stony Brook University. I received my Ph.D. in December 2024, with my dissertation being titled: Understanding the Role of the Internal Combustion Engine for a Hybrid Solid Oxide Fuel Cell Power Generation System. My research focused on internal combustion engines and hybridization with solid oxide fuel cells. The implementation of 3-D computational fluid dynamics (CFD) models for in-house engine geometries and the investigation of the chemical kinetics of hydrogen and carbon monixide combustion are associated with the conducted dissertation. Additional research interests involve the investigation of offshore wind farms, in which both wake and fatigue analysis are examined.
